Welcome & How to Use This Handbook

Welcome to the team. This handbook exists to take you from “I’ve heard of drones” to “I understand how DIMS works, end to end” — without assuming any background in drones, AI, or web software. Read it once, top to bottom, and the rest of your internship will make sense.

Who this is for

You. If you can use a computer and you’re curious, you have enough to start. Every technical word — RTK, YOLO, MQTT, raycast — is explained the first time it appears. You do not need to already know any of them.

How to read it

  1. Go in order, once. The chapters build on each other: foundations first, then hardware, then the software, then the projects. The order is the lesson plan.
  2. After that, treat it as a reference. Jump to any chapter from the sidebar; use the “Filter pages…” box to find one fast.
  3. Don’t memorise — understand. Each chapter ends with a “✅ What you should now be able to say” box. If you can say that paragraph in your own words, you’ve got the chapter.

The map of the handbook

Five parts, twenty-one chapters:

  • Start Here (00–02) — what DIMS is, and the company & projects around it.
  • Foundations (03–06) — drones, software, AI, and 3D/maps, each taught from zero. This is the part that makes everything else readable.
  • The Hardware (07–10) — the Dock 3, the Matrice drones, and the sensors they carry.
  • The Software (11–16) — DJI’s tools, then how the DIMS platform is actually built (backend, frontend, AI).
  • The Projects (17) & Reference (18–20) — the six CNS projects, a glossary, a dev-setup guide, and videos to watch.
💡 A tip for your first week

Read Start Here + Foundations in your first day or two. Then skim the Hardware and Software chapters so you know what exists. Come back and read the chapter that matches whatever you’re assigned in depth. Keep the Glossary open in a tab.

What you’ll be able to do by the end

  • Explain, to a friend, what DIMS does and why it’s useful.
  • Name the main hardware (Dock 3, M4TD) and what each sensor is for.
  • Follow the path of data from the drone, through the backend, to the operator’s screen.
  • Describe how the AI finds a fire and turns it into a map coordinate.
  • Know which of the six projects your work feeds into.
🔒 One housekeeping note

This handbook is a technical onboarding guide. It deliberately contains no commercial or personal information (budgets, contracts, salaries, and the like). For anything in that category, ask your supervisor directly.
