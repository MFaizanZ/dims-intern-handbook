The 6 Projects, Explained

CNS runs six funded projects at once. They are not six separate companies’ worth of work — they are six pieces of one story: building DIMS and proving it in the field. This chapter explains each one and, more importantly, how they fit together.

The shape of it

Think of the six projects in three layers:

  • The platform core — three projects that build the DIMS software, its AI quality bar, and its training data: Deep Tech, TIPS, Data Voucher.
  • The sensor layer — one hardware project building new payloads that feed imagery into DIMS: SWIR.
  • The field applications — two projects that put DIMS to work on a real customer problem: Tower Crane and Forest Fire.
🔑 One sentence

Deep Tech + TIPS + Data Voucher build the DIMS platform; SWIR feeds it new sensors; Tower Crane and Forest Fire run real missions on it.

Layer 1 — The platform core

🧠 Deep Tech — DIMS v2.0 (platform R&D)

The big platform-research project. It builds DIMS Software v2.0 and the AI recognition model v2.0 — the next generation of everything in chapters 12–15. Its scope includes the 3D digital-twin pipeline, an autonomous-flight optimization algorithm, and a five-task work-operation module covering the jobs a drone can do at a site:

cleaningcoatingpaintinginspectionmeasurement

In short: Deep Tech is where the platform itself is pushed forward.

📊 TIPS — DIMS Core AI (the KPIs)

If Deep Tech builds the AI, TIPS defines how good it has to be and proves it. It sets and validates the core-AI performance targets (KPIs) the platform must hit:

KPITarget
Defect-detection accuracy (F1 score)≥ 98.5%
3D digital-twin auto-generation time≤ 20 minutes
Defect 3D-mapping accuracy≥ 95%
3D web-visualization load time≤ 5 seconds

These numbers are why the AI chapter (15) keeps mentioning accuracy and speed — they’re contractual goals, not vanity metrics.

🗂️ Data Voucher — the dataset

Every model in chapter 15 needs training data. This project builds the standardized Drone Facility-Inspection & Cleaning Dataset — on the order of 15 GB and 5,000+ records — that feeds the DIMS AI. It’s organised into parts such as:

  • Infra_Object_Dataset — labelled infrastructure/object imagery.
  • YOLOv8n_TRT_Model — a packaged YOLO / TensorRT model artifact.
  • VLM_Inference_Result — vision-language-model inference outputs.
  • Flight_Simulator_Set & Flight_Log_Dataset — simulated flights and real flight logs.

Layer 2 — The sensor layer

🛰️ SWIR — multi-sensor payload (hardware R&D)

A multi-year hardware project building a drone-mounted integrated sensor payload that combines Multispectral + SWIR + LWIR imaging — plus the quadcopter to carry it. As you learned in chapter 10, SWIR sees things RGB and thermal can’t (moisture, materials), so this payload gives DIMS richer imagery to analyse. It’s the one project that produces new hardware rather than software.

Layer 3 — The field applications

🏗️ Hyundai E&C — Tower Crane Inspection — field application

Drone-based autonomous tower-crane inspection: a drone + AI + 3D twin + a DIMS-linked auto-generated report, applied to inspecting tower cranes. CNS is building the R&D for this on the DIMS platform.

📄 About the Hyundai inspection PDFs

You’ll see Hyundai E&C inspection documents in the project folder. These are outsourced reference data — they’re studied to work out which inspection items a drone should perform, not a deliverable themselves. The core task is organising the drone-inspectable checklist.

🔥 Public Demand Forest Fire — Bomunsan — the first real deployment

A government-funded demonstration of drone-based forest-fire early detection + drone swarm control for Bomunsan (보문산), fully integrated into DIMS. This is the first real DIMS field deployment — the moment everything in this handbook stops being a demo. Its pieces:

  • AI on RGB + thermal imagery (a YOLO Smoke/Fire model);
  • an unmanned charging station (the dock concept) for 24/7 watch;
  • alerting into the city’s disaster-safety platform via API (the ≤8 s coordinatized alert from chapter 15).

How it all connects

Read the chapters you’ve already covered through this lens: the M4TD and sensors capture imagery; the AI (built by Deep Tech, measured by TIPS, trained on Data Voucher) finds and locates problems; SWIR will add new ways to see; and Tower Crane and Forest Fire are the real jobs that prove the whole thing works.
