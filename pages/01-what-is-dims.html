What is DIMS?

DIMS is the product CNS is building. In one sentence: it makes a drone inspect things by itself and tell you exactly what it found and where. This chapter gives you the whole picture before we zoom into any single part.

The problem we’re solving

A huge amount of important infrastructure has to be inspected regularly — bridges, tower cranes, factories, solar farms, power lines, and forests at risk of fire. Today this is mostly done by people: someone climbs the structure, hangs off ropes, or walks the site with a camera. That is slow, expensive, and genuinely dangerous.

🧩 Analogy

Think of a security guard who has to physically walk every floor of a skyscraper every hour, write down anything wrong, and remember exactly where it was. Now imagine replacing that with a camera-equipped robot that does the rounds automatically, recognises problems on its own, and drops a precise pin on a map. That robot is what DIMS orchestrates — except the “robot” is a drone that lives on a rooftop and flies.

Drones can obviously fly up and film these structures. But a raw drone only gives you video. You still need a trained pilot on site, a person watching the feed to spot problems, and a way to convert “I see a crack in this video” into “the crack is at this GPS location.” DIMS automates that entire loop.

What DIMS actually does (end to end)

Here is the full journey, which we’ll spend the rest of the handbook unpacking. Don’t worry about the unfamiliar words — every one is explained later and most have a definition if you hover the dotted terms.

  1. A drone lives in a dock on site. The DJI Dock 3 is a weatherproof box that houses, charges, and launches a Matrice 4TD drone — with no human on site.
  2. DIMS plans a mission. An operator (or a schedule) defines a flight route — a wayline of waypoints, altitudes, and camera actions.
  3. The drone flies it autonomously and streams telemetry (its position, battery, speed…) and live video back to the DIMS cloud.
  4. AI watches the video in real time. Our YOLO models run on a GPU and detect things like fire, smoke, or structural defects, drawing a box around each.
  5. DIMS turns a detection into real coordinates. Using raycasting onto a 3D terrain model, a fire seen as pixels in the video becomes an actual latitude/longitude pin — an alert.
  6. The operator sees everything live in a 3D digital twin in their browser: the drone moving over real terrain, its camera feed with AI boxes, and the coordinatized alerts.
🔑 The one idea to remember

A normal drone gives you footage. DIMS gives you answers with coordinates — automatically, repeatably, with no one in the field. That jump (“pixels → a pin on the map”) is the heart of the product.

Two “modes”: Demo and Real

You’ll hear these constantly, so learn them now. DIMS can run against a simulated drone or a real one — the same software, two backends.

A full software simulator pretends to be a dock and drone. It reports fake-but-realistic telemetry and renders synthetic video. This lets us build, test, and demo the entire system — including AI detection and alerts — with no hardware and zero risk. Most development happens here.

Why it matters

You can do almost all of your work in demo mode. It’s safe, free, and always available.

The same DIMS cloud talks to a real Dock 3 + Matrice 4TD over DJI’s Cloud API. As of June 2026 the real hardware is connected to DIMS (telemetry + control proven; first field deployment coming at Bomunsan).

⚠ Safety

Real hardware can hurt people and itself. There are strict rules — e.g. the real drone must never auto-deploy on a timer. Never point demo behaviour at the real command path.

Want the deep version? How DIMS compares to DJI FlightHub 2

DJI sells its own cloud, FlightHub 2, for managing docks and missions. So why build our own? Because FlightHub is a closed, generic product — you can’t add custom AI, custom 3D twins, custom reports, or integrate it with Korean government disaster platforms. DIMS is CNS’s independent cloud built on the open Cloud API, with the explicit goal of matching and then beating FlightHub 2 on the features that matter for inspection: real-time AI on the live feed, 2D→3D coordinate mapping, and domain-specific workflows (forest fire, tower crane, facility cleaning). That independence is the entire business case.

Where DIMS is headed

DIMS isn’t a lab toy — it has a real first deployment: the Bomunsan (보문산) forest-fire early-detection project in Daejeon, the first time DIMS flies a real mission for a real customer. The other five CNS projects (covered in chapter 17) all feed into or run on this same platform.
